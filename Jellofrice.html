Jellof Rice

description

Jollof Rice is a beloved, smoky, and flavorful West African staple, typically served with protein and a side like plantain or coleslaw.

Ingredients

  • Rice 2 cups
  • Tomatoes (Fresh) 4-5 medium
  • Red Bell Peppers 2
  • Scotch Bonnet/Habanero 1-2
  • Onions 1 large
  • Tomato Paste 2tbsp
  • Stock 3-4 cups
  • Oil 1/4 cup
  • Seasoning Thyme, Curry Powder, Salt, Bouillon Cubes (e.g., Knorr, Maggi)
  • Bay Leaf 1-2

steps

  1. ​Prepare the Pepper Base (Ata Dindin):
    • ​Blend the fresh tomatoes, bell peppers, scotch bonnet, and half an onion until smooth.
    • Chop the remaining half onion and set aside.
  2. Make the Tomato Stew Base:
    • Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat.
    • Sauté the chopped onion until soft (about 3 minutes).
    • Add the tomato paste and fry for 5-10 minutes, stirring constantly, until it darkens (this removes the metallic taste).
    • Pour in the blended pepper mixture. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the stew has reduced and oil floats to the top (about 15-20 minutes). This is your Jollof stew base.
  3. Season the Stew
    • Add the stock, thyme, curry powder, salt, and bouillon cubes to the stew base. Stir well and bring to a rolling boil. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  4. Add the Rice
    • Wash the parboiled rice (rinse until the water runs mostly clear).
    • Stir the washed rice into the boiling stew. Add the bay leaf(ves)
    • The liquid should be slightly above the rice level. If not, add a little more stock or water.
  5. Cook the Jollof
    • Cover the pot tightly with foil/baking paper before placing the lid on top. This traps the steam and creates the essential "smoky" flavor.
    • Reduce heat to very low. Let it cook undisturbed for 20-30 minutes.
  6. Check and Serve
    • Check the rice. If it's still hard, stir gently (without scraping the bottom) and add a splash more liquid, cover, and steam for another 10 minutes.
    • When the rice is tender, fluff gently with a fork. Discard the bay leaf.
    • Serve hot!
